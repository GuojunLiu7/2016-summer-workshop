ISCB DC Area Regional Student Group - Summer 2016 Workshop

June 15, 2016

Welcome to the homepage for the ISCB Washington DC metropolitan area regional student group (RSG) 2016 summer workshop!

One June 15, 2016, we will be hosting a free one-day workshop series with hand-on/open laptop workshops focused on various topics in bioinformatics, computational biology and genomics. The workshop is student driven, with primarily graduate students and post-docs leading the various discussions, but open to anyone interested - students, post-docs, faculty, staff scientists, etc.

There will be several workshop sessions throughout the day, and at each session, attendees can choose from one of two topics of interest to them. For a complete list of workshop topics, see the "schedule" section below.

Location / Getting there

The workshop will take place in the Biosciences Research Building (BRB) on the University of Maryland, College Park campus. We will meet in room 1103 in the in the morning, and for coffee and lunch breaks. The workshops sessions themselves will be held in separate room nearby, as posted on the schedule.

BRB 1103

Biosciences Research Bldg, College Park, MD 20740

Metro

There is a free shuttle bus service that runs between the College Park metro stop on the green line and the University of Maryland campus:

  • Exit from the metro station, and follow the tunnel which leads under the train tracks in the rear.
  • Take the stairs up and look for signs for the 104 Shuttle immediately to your right.
  • Take the bus to the Regents Parking Garage stop (the terminal stop for the bus line).
  • Walk up Fieldhouse Drive and to the left (walking directions.)

Driving

There are metered parking spaces throughout the campus. There nearest location is the Regents Parking Garage, which is a 2-3 minute walk from Biosciences Research Building. See the UMD DOTS website for more information on parking locations and rates.

Contact

If you have any questions about the meeting, you can contact one of the meeting organizers: Keith Hughitt or Thomas Peterson.
