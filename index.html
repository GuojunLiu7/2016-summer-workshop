Welcome to the homepage for the ISCB Washington DC metropolitan area regional student group (RSG) 2016 summer workshop!

On June 15, 2016, we will be hosting a free one-day workshop series with hands-on / open-laptop tutorials focused on various topics in bioinformatics, computational biology and genomics. The workshop is student-driven, with primarily graduate students and post-docs leading the various discussions, but open to anyone interested - students, post-docs, faculty, staff scientists, etc.

Schedule

Workshop talks have been divided into three sessions throughout the day: Morning I, Morning II, and Afternoon I. During each session, participants choose from one of two talks to attend.

Between talks we will reconvene in BRB 1103 for breaks/lunch.

Time Session A Session B
09:00 - 09:30 Welcome and introductions (Eytan Ruppin)
09:30 - 11:00 Introduction to web application development for computational biology (Hiren Karathia) Genome-scale metabolic modeling (Joo Sang Lee)
11:00 - 11:30 Coffee Break
11:30 - 01:00 Bacterial genome assembly & annotation (Jonathan Goodson) Analyzing sequence motifs through maximum entropy modeling (Patrick O'neill)
01:00 - 02:00 Lunch
02:00 - 03:30 Comparative genomics of transcriptional regulation in Bacteria (Sefa Kilic) Co-expression network analysis using RNA-Seq data (Keith Hughitt)
03:30 - 04:00 Closing Remarks

There will also be a social event planned for after the meeting on or nearby campus - details will be shared at the meeting.

Location / Getting there

The workshop will take place in the Biosciences Research Building (BRB) on the University of Maryland, College Park campus. We will meet in room 1103 in the in the morning, and for coffee and lunch breaks. The workshop sessions will take place in one of two locations in the same building. See the schedule section for details.

BRB 1103
Biosciences Research Bldg, College Park, MD 20740

Metro

There is a free shuttle bus service that runs between the College Park metro stop on the green line and the University of Maryland campus:

  • Exit from the metro station, and follow the tunnel which leads under the train tracks in the rear.
  • Take the stairs up and look for signs for the 104 Shuttle immediately to your right.
  • Take the bus to the Regents Parking Garage stop (the terminal stop for the bus line).
  • Walk up Fieldhouse Drive and to the left (walking directions.)

Driving

There are metered parking spaces throughout the campus. There nearest location is the Regents Parking Garage, which is a 2-3 minute walk from Biosciences Research Building. See the UMD DOTS website for more information on parking locations and rates.
